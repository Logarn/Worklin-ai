# Public Source Map

Prefer sources in this order, while recording what was actually available:

1. Official brand pages, product pages, policies, public social profiles, and
   public filings.
2. Named competitor pages and their official public channels.
3. Independent reviews, reputable trade press, interviews, and market reports.
4. Search results and snippets as discovery leads only.
5. Analyst inference, clearly labeled and never presented as observed fact.

For email and SMS, public signup surfaces reveal positioning and capture
promises, not private campaign performance. For social, a sample of public
posts reveals observable themes and formats, not a complete strategy. For
investor sentiment, public market commentary is not a substitute for verified
financial data. When a source is blocked, stale, ambiguous, or inaccessible,
write that down as a gap.
