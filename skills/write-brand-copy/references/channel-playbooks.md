## RIVET

Use RIVET as a flexible construction sequence:

1. **Recognize:** name the situation, job, workaround, or triggering moment.
2. **Introduce bounded tension:** contrast expectation and reality while defining the subject and payoff.
3. **Deliver value early:** give the first useful answer before asking for attention or action.
4. **Establish belief:** show mechanism, demonstration, scoped proof, limitations, and objection resolution.
5. **Transition to action:** make the CTA a natural continuation of the argument.

Do not force every asset into five visible sections. Preserve the functions while adapting length and order to the channel.

## Email

Use:

- subject: specific relevance plus a concrete benefit or bounded tension
- preheader: new information, not a repetition
- opening: immediate payoff of the subject
- body: one primary idea developed through mechanism, example, proof, or objection resolution
- CTA: one primary action unless the email is intentionally navigational

Prioritize behavioral and contextual personalization over first-name insertion. Do not imply knowledge of private thoughts.

Evaluate delivered messages by qualified clicks, conversion, revenue or pipeline, unsubscribe, and spam complaints. Treat opens as a noisy diagnostic.

## Organic Social

Use this sequence:

1. recognize a familiar problem or belief
2. introduce a non-obvious proposition
3. demonstrate or provide evidence
4. explain the mechanism
5. give an applicable takeaway
6. offer an optional next action

Give the post a real sharing affordance: utility, identity, social connection, emotion, or status. Do not promise virality.

## Paid Social

Align ad, audience, and landing destination. Use one main angle and ensure the destination fulfills the opening promise.

Do not assert or imply sensitive personal attributes about the viewer. Avoid negative self-perception tactics, unrealistic outcomes, and sensational claims. Keep disclosures and material terms visible enough for the channel.

Test qualified action and landing-page behavior, not click-through rate alone.

## Landing Pages And Ecommerce

Build in this order when appropriate:

1. useful desired outcome
2. recognition of the customer's situation
3. mechanism and differentiation
4. demonstration
5. verified proof
6. primary objection resolution
7. offer contents, price, delivery, and conditions
8. precise risk reduction
9. verified urgency or scarcity with its operational reason
10. proportionate CTA

Make the product causally responsible for the resolution. Do not attach an unrelated emotional story to a logo at the end.

For high-involvement decisions, add implementation requirements, comparison logic, limitations, switching considerations, risk reduction, and financial or operational justification.

## Short-Form Video

Use this starting structure as a hypothesis, not a timing law:

- opening: visible evidence, a specific contradiction, or a recognizable moment
- promise: define what the viewer will understand or do
- mechanism: explain the causal idea in coherent beats
- demonstration: show the screen, object, process, comparison, or result
- payoff: resolve the opening tension exactly
- action: offer a proportionate next step

Generate hooks from different families:

- audience recognition
- counterintuitive claim
- demonstration first
- verified result
- mistake diagnosis
- before-and-after contrast
- specific prediction
- story in progress
- consequence or hidden cost
- open question with a defined payoff

Do not add arbitrary pattern interrupts. Entertainment must support attention, explanation, memory, or product integration.

Evaluate opening retention, retention at concept boundaries, completion relative to length, qualified action, and landing-page conversion.

## Educational And Long-Form Video

Use:

> Question -> Prediction -> Explanation -> Example -> Non-example -> Application -> Retrieval

Reveal the teaching value early. Use signaling, coherent segmentation, and visual demonstration. Remove jokes, stories, or edits that compete with comprehension.

Treat shorter and longer scripts as testable formats. Preserve the mechanism and proof required for the audience's involvement level instead of cutting to an arbitrary duration.
