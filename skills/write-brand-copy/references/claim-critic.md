## Claim Ledger

Classify material claims before drafting:

- **VERIFIED:** supported by supplied evidence within the stated scope
- **QUALIFIED INFERENCE:** reasonable but must retain explicit limits
- **ASPIRATIONAL:** a possible or intended outcome, not a promise
- **UNSUPPORTED:** remove from final copy

Require a ledger entry for quantitative, comparative, testimonial, health, earnings, performance, demand, scarcity, urgency, certification, media, and superlative claims.

## Two-Pass Review

### Copy pass

Check whether:

- the opening identifies a real audience situation
- the payoff resolves the exact hook
- the central proposition is understandable on first pass
- abstract adjectives have become observable detail
- the mechanism connects product and outcome
- emotion advances the argument
- the primary objection is resolved
- the CTA fits readiness and preserves agency
- the language matches the approved speaker and examples

### Claim and compliance pass

Check every material statement against its ledger entry. Confirm scope, date, source, qualification, comparison set, disclosures, real availability, and destination consistency.

Escalate jurisdiction-specific or regulated claims for appropriate review. The skill is a guardrail, not legal approval.

## Scoring Rubric

Score each dimension from 0 to 5:

- relevance
- hook-payoff alignment
- clarity
- specificity
- mechanism
- proof
- product integration
- objection handling
- offer clarity
- CTA fit
- emotional progression
- voice authenticity
- information density
- autonomy
- compliance

Do not approve based on a high average. Any hard rejection fails the artifact. Treat a score below 3 in proof, compliance, hook-payoff alignment, or offer clarity as a blocking issue for commercial copy.

## Hard Rejection Rules

Remove or rewrite:

- fabricated reviews, testimonials, quotations, counts, or case studies
- invented statistics, research, certifications, or media coverage
- fake deadlines, inventory, waitlists, demand, or resetting countdowns
- unqualified guarantees or universal performance claims
- scientific claims without appropriately scoped evidence
- comparative, number-one, or best claims without support and a defined comparison
- customer quotations modified while presented as verbatim
- unsupported before-and-after claims
- deceptive hook-body or ad-destination mismatch
- hidden material terms
- paid-ad copy asserting sensitive personal attributes
- shame, coercion, or fear without a credible response
- any claim rewritten at a higher confidence than its evidence

## Language Challenges

Challenge, but do not mechanically ban:

`revolutionary`, `game-changing`, `transformative`, `unmatched`, `best`, `number one`, `scientifically proven`, `guaranteed`, `instant`, `effortless`, `risk-free`, `everyone`, `always`, `never`, `secret`, `ultimate`, `once-in-a-lifetime`, `act now`, `you need this`, `unlock`, `elevate`, `supercharge`, `seamless`, `powerful`, and `amazing`.

Ask what function the phrase performs and whether mechanism, evidence, or specificity could perform it better.

Use causal language only when the explanation adds information. Use social proof only when the count, date, population, and status are accurate. Use scarcity only when the constraint and its operational reason are verified.

Pair pain or threat with efficacy. Prefer direct, choice-preserving CTAs over forceful imperatives when reactance risk is high.
