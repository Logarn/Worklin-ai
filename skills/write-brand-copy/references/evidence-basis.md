# Evidence Basis

Use research as a prior, not a substitute for the brand's controlled audience data.

## Strong Default Foundations

- Prefer concrete language and observable mechanisms over unsupported abstraction. See the [Journal of Consumer Research field evidence](https://academic.oup.com/jcr/article-abstract/47/5/787/5873524).
- Keep hooks, bodies, and payoffs aligned; use bounded curiosity rather than cryptic clickbait. See the [uncertainty and curiosity experiments](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-93464-6).
- Use relevant, accurately described social proof instead of generic popularity. See the [social-norm meta-analysis](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/00222429211029199).
- Use real scarcity and distinguish demand, supply, and time mechanisms. See the [scarcity meta-analysis](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0022435922000434).
- Pair threat with efficacy and agency. See the [fear-appeal meta-analysis](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26501228/).
- Preserve instructional coherence in educational media. See the [Cambridge Handbook of Multimedia Learning](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/cambridge-handbook-of-multimedia-learning/principles-for-reducing-extraneous-processing-in-multimedia-learning-coherence-signaling-redundancy-spatial-contiguity-and-temporal-contiguity-principles/CD5B7AE1279A9AB81F8EEBB53DBEC86E).
- Keep claims truthful, non-deceptive, and supported before dissemination. See [FTC advertising guidance](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/advertising-marketing).

## Contextual Techniques To Test

Treat negativity, first-name personalization, humor, narrative, slang, fear intensity, curiosity, imperative CTAs, scarcity type, emotional framing, and exact length as hypotheses.

Examples:

- Negative words increased clicks in a large news-headline setting, but that does not establish higher product sales. See the [Upworthy headline experiments](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41562-023-01538-4).
- Moderate syntactic surprise can outperform language that is too predictable or too incoherent. See the [Journal of Marketing research](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/abs/10.1177/00222429231153582).
- First-name email personalization has not produced a consistently positive effect across replications. See the [2023 replication](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11002-023-09701-7).
- Emotion can aid sharing, while useful information and product integration can matter more for evaluation and purchase. See the [valuable virality research](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/pdf/10.1509/jmr.13.0350).
- Structured human-model collaboration can be more useful than one-shot ghostwriting and helps reduce anchoring. See the [LLM advertising workflow research](https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2023.03014).

## Reject As Universal Rules

Reject claims that a fixed word list always converts, `because` creates compliance, first names always increase opens, short or long copy always wins, stories always sell better than facts, negative hooks always outperform, arbitrary pattern interrupts are mandatory, or any hook can guarantee virality.

Update these priors with dated results from the same audience, channel, offer, and metric. Record confounds and avoid learning from raw attention when the goal is qualified action.
